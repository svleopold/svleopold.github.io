Paines Prairie Preserve State Park

In February, 2017 I had the opportunity to visit Paynes Prairie Preserve State Park. This Florida State Park, a U.S. National Natural Landmark, encompasses a 21,000-acre (savanna  south of Gainesville. It is also a U.S. National Natural Landmark. It is in the center of the Paynes Prairie Basin which is primarily drained by the Alachua Sink.The park is a preserved wilderness, inhabited by natural fauna such as birds, alligators and bison. It also has small herds of Florida Cracker horses and cattle, originally introduced by the Seminoles. The park itself is a recreation of pre-settler Florida and is in marked contrast to the tourist areas of the state.
